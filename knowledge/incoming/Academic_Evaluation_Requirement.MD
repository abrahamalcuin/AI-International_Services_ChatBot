# Academic Evaluation Requirement

## Overview
This page outlines the academic evaluation requirements for international applicants to BYU-Idaho. It includes accepted evaluation agencies, documentation rules based on earned credits, timing recommendations, policies for students with extensive U.S. college credit, and transfer equivalency processes. Students should review this information before submitting transcripts or ordering an evaluation.

---

## Essential Information

**Accepted Academic Evaluators**  
BYU-Idaho accepts transcript evaluations from **International Education Research Foundation (IERF)** and **TEC (formerly SpanTran)**. These services operate under a joint agreement with BYU, BYU-Hawaii, and Ensign College. References on agency websites may list BYU generally, but the evaluations apply to BYU-Idaho.

Choosing an alternate evaluator may result in added fees and processing delays. Applicants should submit documents to IERF or TEC **4–6 weeks before the admissions deadline** to ensure timely delivery to CES.

**Documentation Requirements by Credit Level**  
- Applicants with **fewer than 24 college semester credits** must submit both **secondary education records** and any **college coursework completed outside the U.S.**  
- Applicants with **24 or more credits completed outside the U.S.** must submit only their **international college coursework** for evaluation.

**IERF**  
IERF is the preferred CES evaluation agency. Applicants should select the **Detail Report** option when ordering an evaluation.

**TEC**  
TEC provides a CES-specific evaluation portal. Applicants should follow the instructions to request a **course analysis** for non-U.S. transcripts.

**International Students With 90+ U.S. Credits**  
International students with **90 or more credits from a U.S. college or university** are **not eligible** to apply for on-campus study. This includes credits earned through BYU-Idaho Online, Pathway, and Institute. These students may continue their studies through BYU-Pathway Worldwide programs.

International students who previously studied on campus at BYU-Idaho and have since exceeded 90 U.S. credits are **eligible to reapply.**

**Transfer Equivalency After Admission**  
Once admitted, university credits must undergo transfer equivalency review. Students should consult BYU-Idaho's international transfer credit site for details.

Source Link: https://www.byui.edu/admissions/apply/international-students/finances

## Deadlines, Requirements, & Criteria

- **Deadline 1:** Not applicable  
- **Deadline 2:** Not applicable  
- **Requirement / Rule:** Applicants must use IERF or TEC for academic evaluations and must submit required documents based on their completed credits. Students with 90+ U.S. credits may not apply for on-campus study.
