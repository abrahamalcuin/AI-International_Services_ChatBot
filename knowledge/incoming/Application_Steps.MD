# Application Steps

## Overview
This page explains the application process for international students seeking to study on campus at BYU-Idaho. It includes eligibility rules based on completed U.S. college credits, required application steps, visa procedures, SEVIS fee details, and check-in expectations. Students should begin preparing well in advance of the application deadline.

---

## Essential Information

**Eligibility: International Students With 90+ U.S. Credits**  
International students with **90 or more credits earned at any U.S. college or university** are **not eligible** to apply for on-campus study. This includes credits earned through **BYU-Idaho Online, Pathway, and Institute** programs. Students in this situation are encouraged to continue their education through **BYU-Pathway Worldwide**.  
International students who previously studied **on campus at BYU-Idaho** and have since reached 90+ credits **may reapply**.

---

**Application Steps (Start 6 Months Before the Deadline)**

1. **Begin Application**  
   Start your online application using the Apply Now link.  
   Submit the application before the following deadlines:  
   - **Fall:** May 1  
   - **Winter:** September 15  
   - **Spring:** January 15

2. **Deposit**  
   Pay the required international student deposit. Details are available on the **Finances** page.

3. **Mail I-20 Document**  
   BYU-Idaho will mail your Form I-20, which is required to apply for an F-1 visa and to enter the United States.

4. **Pay SEVIS I-901 Fee**  
   Pay the SEVIS fee **at least 3 days** before your visa appointment or, for Canadians, before arriving at the U.S. border.  
   - F-1 Visa: **$350**  
   - J-1 Visa: **$220**

5. **Go to U.S. Embassy for F-1 Visa**  
   Schedule a visa interview at your nearest U.S. embassy or consulate.  
   Bring your **I-901 payment receipt**.  
   *Note: Canadian students do not need to apply for an F-1 visa; it is waived at the border.*

6. **Arrive at School & Check In**  
   You may enter the United States **up to 30 days before** the program start date listed on your I-20.  
   Check in with **International Services** within **two weeks** of the semester start date.

---

## Deadlines, Requirements, & Criteria

- **Deadline 1:** Fall – May 1  
- **Deadline 2:** Winter – September 15  
- **Requirement / Rule:** Students must follow all application steps, pay required fees, and remain eligible for on-campus study (including the 90-credit rule).
